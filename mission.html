Our Mission



Gas stations outnumber public charging stations by around seven to one.
This causes many to question the practicality of driving an electric car.
Studies show that consumers are more likely to buy an electric car when they
see nearby stations in town. The insufficient amount of charging stations
is a real concern that is deterring some consumers from going all-electric.
Access to efficient charging is now a roadblock to electric-vehicle uptake.
The problem being the charging infrastructure lacks a viable business model
and the plan to radically expand the number of available charging stations
depends on exponential growth in the sale of EVs. We have gathered data
to show that despite many roadblocks EV sales have increased dramatically and will
continue to do so in the coming years. Going electric is not just an eco-friendly goal.
It's a business reality, and a major transition in the industry. It is the Future.
